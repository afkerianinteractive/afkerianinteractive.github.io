3. SUMMARY OF DATA PRACTICES

Tap Odyssey:

- does not require or permit the creation of an account administered by AFKERIAN INTERACTIVE;
- does not use its own servers to store or synchronize player profiles;
- stores the progress, preferences, and ordinary state of the Game primarily on the Android device;
- does not request, during ordinary use, the name, postal address, contacts, telephone number, identity documents, financial information, medical information, or biometric data;
- uses Google AdMob to display advertising;
- uses Google User Messaging Platform to administer certain advertising privacy messages and options;
- uses Google Analytics for Firebase;
- uses Firebase Crashlytics;
- uses Firebase Installations;
- uses Firebase Sessions; and
- receives information directly from the user when the user voluntarily contacts support or submits a privacy request.

The absence of first-party accounts and servers does not mean that no data leaves the device. The Google services integrated in the Game may automatically transmit and process certain technical data, identifiers, interactions, diagnostics, and IP addresses.


4. ABSENCE OF USER ACCOUNTS

Tap Odyssey does not require registration, a user name, a password, or a sign-in.

AFKERIAN INTERACTIVE does not create or administer player accounts and does not maintain a first-party database of user profiles.

Therefore:

- there is no AFKERIAN INTERACTIVE account that the user must delete;
- there is no activity history linked to a first-party Game account;
- there is no game profile stored on first-party servers; and
- no sign-in information is used to access the Game.

6. DELETION OF LOCAL DATA

The user may delete the local data of Tap Odyssey through the controls available within the Game, where they exist, or through the Android settings:

1. Open the Android settings.
2. Go to the applications section.
3. Select Tap Odyssey.
4. Open the storage section.
5. Select the option to clear the application's data or storage.

The user may also delete the local data by uninstalling the Game.

Clearing only the cache may not delete all local progress, settings, or state.

Android backup systems may restore previously backed-up information after a reinstallation or a device change. The user may administer that behavior through the backup configuration of Android, Google, and the device manufacturer.
